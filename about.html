I understand healthcare from the front line through executive strategy.

My career has been a deliberate climb through the operating layers of a health system — registration, analytics, governance, performance improvement, and enterprise transformation. That range is the point.

Professional Story

A career built one operating layer at a time

I began my healthcare career at the point of care, as an emergency department registrar and Epic super user. Standing between a patient and the system taught me something I have never unlearned: access, patient experience, and financial performance are not separate problems. They are the same problem, viewed from different seats.

From there I followed the operations upstream. As a call center analytics intern and then a workforce analyst, I learned how scheduling, staffing, and demand shape whether patients can actually get care. As an enterprise senior compliance operations analyst, I saw how governance and accountability protect an organization's ability to perform at all. As an operating effectiveness lead analyst, I led the work of turning data into measurable operational change. And as an EHR revenue cycle analyst, I helped move enterprise-scale technology transformation — the place where people, process, and technology either align or fail together.

Today I also teach. As an adjunct professor, I help develop the next generation of healthcare administrators — which has sharpened how I think, communicate, and lead. The through-line across all of it is simple: I make healthcare operations work better, and I build the people and systems that keep them working.

Leadership Philosophy

Servant leadership, applied to operations

I believe leadership is the work of multiplying capability — clearing obstacles, creating clarity, and developing the people and processes around you so the result outlasts your involvement.

Serve the people doing the work

The best operating insight usually lives at the front line. My job is to listen for it and remove what's in its way.

Make the right thing the easy thing

Sustainable performance comes from well-designed processes, not from heroics or pressure.

Decide with evidence

Data earns its keep only when it changes a decision and improves an outcome.

Lead change with people, not at them

Transformation succeeds when people understand the why and own the how.

Career Vision

Where I'm going

I am building toward senior operating leadership — director, vice president, and chief operating roles, and consulting leadership — where I can shape how a health system delivers access, manages cost, and performs financially across the whole enterprise.

My aim is to lead transformation that is durable: operations that run well after the project ends, teams that grow stronger, and technology that actually serves the people using it. I want to be the kind of executive who is equally credible explaining a workflow to a registrar and a margin to a board.
