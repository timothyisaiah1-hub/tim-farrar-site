Six chapters of leadership development

This isn't a list of jobs. It's the deliberate progression of capability — each role adding a layer to how I see, measure, and move a healthcare organization. Select any chapter to expand it.

+ Chapter 1 · The Front Line
Emergency Department Registrar & Epic Super User
Employer · Dates · Understanding healthcare at the point of care
The Challenge

Serve patients at one of the most stressful moments in healthcare — emergency registration — while capturing the accurate access, demographic, and financial information the entire downstream system depends on.

Responsibilities
  • Registered and supported patients arriving for emergency care
  • Served as Epic super user — supporting peers, troubleshooting workflows, and reinforcing adoption
  • Captured accurate access and revenue cycle data at the point of entry
  • Communicated with patients, families, and clinical staff under pressure
Competencies Developed
  • Customer service & patient experience
  • Adaptability under pressure
  • Front-line operations fluency
  • Healthcare access awareness
Lessons Learned
  • The data captured at registration shapes everything downstream — access, claims, and cash
  • Empathy and operational accuracy are not in tension; they reinforce each other
  • System adoption lives or dies with the people on the floor
Impact Created
  • Strengthened front-line Epic adoption as a super user
  • Improved registration accuracy at the point of care
  • Built the operating intuition that informs all my later work
“My healthcare leadership journey began at the point of care, where I learned that access, patient experience, and financial performance are deeply connected.”
+ Chapter 2 · How Systems Operate
Call Center Analytics Intern → Workforce Analyst
Employer · Dates · Learning how healthcare systems operate
The Challenge

Move from serving one patient at a time to understanding the system — why access breaks, where demand and capacity collide, and how analytics can expose the bottleneck.

Responsibilities
  • Analyzed call center performance, access, and scheduling data
  • Supported workforce optimization and staffing-to-demand modeling
  • Contributed to a Genesys contact-center implementation
  • Supported Epic integration efforts across access operations
Competencies Developed
  • Data-driven decision making
  • Systems thinking
  • Workforce management
  • Process improvement
Lessons Learned
  • Most access problems are operations problems wearing a clinical costume
  • The right metric reframes the entire conversation
  • Technology change is really workforce change
Impact Created
  • Contributed to reduced call abandonment rates
  • Supported a Genesys implementation and Epic integration efforts
  • Helped surface scheduling and staffing improvements
“I learned how analytics can uncover operational bottlenecks and improve access to care.”
+ Chapter 3 · Governance & Risk
Enterprise Senior Compliance Operations Analyst
Employer · Dates · Governance and organizational risk
The Challenge

Protect the performance of a large enterprise by building the governance, accountability, and risk infrastructure that lets thousands of people operate consistently and safely.

Responsibilities
  • Led and supported enterprise compliance operations
  • Supported a Navex governance/ethics platform implementation
  • Contributed to AI governance program development
  • Helped coordinate a Code of Conduct rollout across the enterprise
Competencies Developed
  • Executive communication
  • Risk management
  • Program leadership
  • Stakeholder influence
Lessons Learned
  • Governance is what makes performance repeatable at scale
  • Influence without authority is the core executive skill
  • Emerging technology like AI needs guardrails built in, not bolted on
Impact Created
  • Supported a Navex implementation across enterprise operations
  • Contributed to AI governance and risk frameworks
  • Helped deliver a Code of Conduct rollout reaching approximately 42,000 employees
“Sustainable organizational performance requires governance, accountability, and operational discipline.”
+ Chapter 4 · Operational Transformation
Operating Effectiveness Lead Analyst
Employer · Dates · Turning data into operational change
The Challenge

Close the gap between insight and action — taking performance data and Lean Six Sigma method and converting them into measurable operational and financial improvement.

Responsibilities
  • Led operating effectiveness and performance improvement initiatives
  • Applied Lean Six Sigma to workflow redesign
  • Built productivity dashboards for operational leaders
  • Drove cost savings and financial stewardship initiatives
Competencies Developed
  • Process redesign
  • Change leadership
  • Operational excellence
  • Continuous improvement
Lessons Learned
  • Data only creates value when it drives a decision and a change
  • Dashboards are tools for action, not trophies for display
  • Durable improvement requires fixing the process, not the symptom
Impact Created
  • Contributed to an approximately 55% reduction in automated dispensing errors
  • Developed productivity dashboards adopted by operational leaders
  • Supported measurable cost savings initiatives
“Data only creates value when it drives operational change.”
+ Chapter 5 · Enterprise Transformation
EHR Revenue Cycle Analyst
Employer · Dates · Aligning people, process, and technology
The Challenge

Help move an enterprise-scale Epic implementation and revenue cycle transformation — the highest-stakes kind of change, where clinical, operational, financial, and technical worlds all have to land at once.

Responsibilities
  • Supported an enterprise Epic implementation
  • Drove revenue cycle optimization across Resolute HB workflows
  • Contributed to operational readiness and testing governance
  • Engaged stakeholders across clinical, operational, and technical teams
Competencies Developed
  • Program execution
  • Enterprise change management
  • Technology transformation
  • Strategic alignment
Lessons Learned
  • Large-scale transformation is an alignment problem before it is a technology problem
  • Testing governance and readiness discipline prevent expensive go-live failures
  • The revenue cycle is where strategy meets reality — and cash
Impact Created
  • Supported enterprise Epic implementation and operational readiness
  • Contributed to revenue cycle transformation outcomes
  • Strengthened testing governance across the program
“Large-scale transformation succeeds when people, process, and technology are aligned.”
+ Chapter 6 · Developing Future Leaders
Adjunct Professor, Rowan University
Dates · Multiplying capability in others
The Challenge

Translate a decade of operating experience into the development of future healthcare administrators — and in doing so, sharpen my own executive thinking and communication.

Responsibilities
  • Teach healthcare administration coursework at the university level
  • Develop curriculum, case studies, and assessments
  • Mentor and coach emerging healthcare professionals
  • Connect academic frameworks to real operating practice
Competencies Developed
  • Communication
  • Influence
  • Talent development
  • Executive presence
Lessons Learned
  • If you can teach it clearly, you understand it deeply
  • Developing others is the highest-leverage thing a leader does
  • The next generation reframes your own assumptions
Impact Created
  • Developing future healthcare administrators and leaders
  • Building durable thought leadership and executive communication
  • Strengthening the talent pipeline for the field
“Leadership is multiplying capability in others.”
